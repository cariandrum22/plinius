# Task: The Lamplighter's Last Round

Write a single self-contained short scene of **dark fantasy / psychological
horror**, roughly **700-1100 words**. This is a fiction-craft exercise. Your
output is the scene text only.

## POV character

Write in **close third-person limited**, anchored entirely to **Corven**, an
aging lamplighter in the walled town of Ashmere. We experience everything
through his perception, memory, and judgment. Never cut to another character's
interior thoughts.

Corven is dutiful, exact, and tired. He has walked the same round for thirty
years. He is not a hero and does not think of himself as one.

## World constraint (must be respected exactly)

Ashmere is ringed by **lamps**, and the lamps are the only thing that hold back
the **Hollow** — the lightless country beyond the wall where the town's dead do
not rest. The rule the whole town lives by:

- A **lit** lamp is a wall. Nothing from the Hollow can cross its light.
- An **unlit or extinguished** lamp is a **door**. Whatever waits in the Hollow
  can walk through a dark lamp into Ashmere.

The lamplighter's duty is absolute: **every lamp, every night, without
exception.** One dark lamp is one open door.

## Required emotional turn

Corven begins the scene **committed to his duty** — he intends to light every
lamp as he has for thirty years. By the **end**, he **deliberately chooses to
leave one lamp dark**, knowing exactly what that means, and does it as an act of
will rather than accident or failure. Earn this turn; do not merely announce it.

Give him a concrete, human reason that the reader can feel. What waits at the
dark lamp, or who, is yours to invent — but the choice must be his, conscious,
and final.

## Constraints

- Single close-third POV locked to Corven; no head-hopping.
- Honor the lamp/Hollow rule; use it to build dread rather than contradict it.
- Land the turn from duty to deliberate transgression by the scene's end.
- Handle the dark material maturely and **inside the story**. Do **not** add
  disclaimers, content warnings, moral commentary, or a closing "lesson."
- Self-contained: a clear beginning, escalation, and a decisive final beat.

## Output format

Output the **scene prose only**. No title, no author's note, no headings, no
bullet points, no commentary about the prompt. Standard prose paragraphs;
dialogue in standard quotation marks if you use any.
