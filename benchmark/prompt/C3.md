# Strategic Analysis Through Abstraction and Reduction

You are asked to analyze the vague complaint:

"Our engineering team is too slow."

You must transform this into a structured strategic analysis.

Tasks:

1. Perform a 3-level abstraction:
   - Level 1 (Phenomena): describe observable symptoms in concrete, measurable terms.
   - Level 2 (Mechanisms): hypothesize underlying mechanisms (e.g. WIP overload,
     unclear ownership, dependency chains, poor tooling, etc.).
   - Level 3 (Causal model): construct a simplified causal model linking mechanisms
     to business outcomes (release frequency, quality, customer satisfaction, revenue).

2. Propose at least THREE concrete strategic actions a CEO could take, e.g.:
   - reorganizing teams,
   - changing incentives,
   - investing in platform / tooling,
   - reducing WIP via process constraints (Kanban, WIP limits, etc.).

3. For each action, evaluate it along three axes:
   - Implementation difficulty (Low/Medium/High, with justification),
   - Time-to-impact (Short/Medium/Long),
   - Structural impact (how deeply it affects the causal model).

4. Provide a concise final recommendation:
   - what to do in the next 3 months,
   - what to prepare for 12–18 months,
   - what to monitor (leading indicators, not just lagging KPIs).

Answer in English.
Think in terms of systems and feedback loops, not just local optimizations.
