# Constrained Alpha Construction

You are designing cross-sectional alpha signals for a hypothetical equity market.

Assume:

- Universe: 100 stocks.
- Factors: 3 systematic factors: Market, Size, Momentum.
- You can construct a daily-rebalanced long-short portfolio.
- Constraints:
  - Net exposure ≈ 0 (market neutral).
  - Gross exposure ≤ 1.8.
  - Turnover should be kept reasonably low.
  - You want to design alphas that do not rely on unrealistic frictionless assumptions.
- There is regime dependence: sometimes Momentum breaks down; sometimes Size becomes dominant.

Your task:

1. Propose at least THREE distinct alpha construction ideas under these constraints, for example:
   - pure cross-sectional ranking-based,
   - residual-based after factor regression,
   - regime-switching or conditional alpha.

2. For each alpha:
   - Specify what input data it needs (prices, volumes, fundamentals, etc.).
   - Explain how it is constructed mathematically (at least at the pseudo-code or formula level).
   - Explain in which market regimes it is expected to work well or fail.
   - Explain how you would limit maximum drawdown to around 20% at the portfolio level
     (risk controls, position sizing, volatility targeting, etc.).

3. Propose a backtesting and evaluation protocol:
   - in-sample / out-of-sample split,
   - what performance metrics to use (Sharpe, Sortino, max DD, etc.),
   - how to avoid overfitting and data snooping.

Answer in English and think step by step.
Structure the answer with clear headings and bullet points.
