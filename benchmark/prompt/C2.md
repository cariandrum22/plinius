# Causal Modeling of Business Metrics

You are modeling a SaaS business with annual revenue:

Revenue = R _ ARPU _ Retention

where:

- R: number of active paying accounts
- ARPU: average revenue per user
- Retention: 1 - churn_rate

Assume current baseline:

- R = 10,000
- ARPU = 100
- Retention = 0.90

Three strategic levers for the next 12 months:

- L1: Increase ARPU via packaging and upsell
- L2: Increase R via marketing and sales
- L3: Improve Retention via product and CS

You have the following rough, uncertain impacts (mean effects):

- L1: +15% ARPU, but risk of -3% absolute drop in Retention if executed poorly.
- L2: +30% R, but requires extra marketing spend that effectively reduces net margin by 10%.
- L3: +5 percentage points in Retention (from 0.90 to 0.95), but with no direct effect on ARPU.

Tasks:

1. Build a simple causal model (graph + equations) relating:
   - R, ARPU, Retention,
   - the three levers L1, L2, L3,
   - and net profit (not just revenue).

2. For three scenarios (A, B, C), propose different combinations of L1, L2, L3:
   - Scenario A: aggressive growth,
   - Scenario B: balanced,
   - Scenario C: conservative but resilient.

3. For each scenario:
   - estimate the new R, ARPU, Retention,
   - compute the resulting Revenue,
   - reason qualitatively about net profit and risk.

4. Prioritize the levers for a rational CEO:
   - which lever should be executed first,
   - which should be piloted or tested with guardrails,
   - which might be deferred.

Answer in English and show your reasoning steps and any simple math you use.
