# Portfolio Risk Decomposition with Dummy Covariance

You are given a 4-asset portfolio with the following weights:

- Asset 1 (large-cap equity): w1 = 0.40
- Asset 2 (small-cap equity): w2 = 0.25
- Asset 3 (corporate bonds): w3 = 0.20
- Asset 4 (commodity futures): w4 = 0.15

Assume the following annualized volatilities (σ) and correlations (ρ):

σ1 = 20%, σ2 = 24%, σ3 = 8%, σ4 = 18%

Correlation matrix ρ:

        1     2     3     4

1 1.00 0.85 0.30 0.40
2 0.85 1.00 0.25 0.45
3 0.30 0.25 1.00 0.10
4 0.40 0.45 0.10 1.00

Tasks:

1. Construct the covariance matrix Σ from the volatilities and correlations.
2. Compute (symbolically, not numerically) the portfolio variance wᵀ Σ w.
3. Conceptually decompose the portfolio risk into:
   - factor-related risk (e.g., equity beta, size, term spread, commodity beta),
   - idiosyncratic risk,
   - contributions by asset and by factor.

4. Explain how regime changes (e.g., equity bear market, credit crunch, commodity shock)
   would change:
   - the covariance matrix structure,
   - the risk contribution of each asset,
   - the suitability of this portfolio for a risk-averse investor.

5. Propose a systematic way to monitor and report this risk decomposition over time
   to a risk committee.

Answer in English.
Focus on the conceptual framework and formulas, not just plugging numbers.
