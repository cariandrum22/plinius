# Decision Tree Under Uncertainty

You are the CEO of a SaaS company considering launching a new AI-based product.

Assume the following simplified numbers (all in arbitrary units):

- Base development cost today: 5M
- Additional scaling cost if product succeeds: 3M
- Possible annual net profit if successful: 4M per year (for 5 years)
- Possible annual net profit if partially successful: 1.5M per year (for 5 years)
- Loss if product fails (including opportunity cost): -2M total

Uncertainties (rough prior beliefs):

- Market adoption:
  - High adoption: 30%
  - Medium adoption: 40%
  - Low adoption: 30%

- Competitive pressure:
  - Strong competition: reduces payoffs by 40%
  - Moderate competition: reduces payoffs by 20%
  - Weak competition: reduces payoffs by 0%

- Regulatory risk:
  - No major regulation: 80%
  - Restrictive future regulation: 20% (reduces expected lifetime to 3 years)

Tasks:

1. Build a qualitative decision tree for the "Launch" vs "Do not launch" decision,
   explicitly modeling:
   - stages of decision,
   - chance nodes (adoption, competition, regulation),
   - payoff at terminal nodes.

2. Compute or at least symbolically represent the expected value of "Launch"
   under these assumptions.

3. Discuss how:
   - real options (e.g. staging investment, pilot launch, geographic limitation),
   - information value (e.g. running a paid pilot),
     could change your decision tree and the decision today.

4. Provide a clear recommendation, with reasoning, from the point of view
   of a risk-aware CEO.

Answer in English with clear structure and, if helpful, simple formulas.
